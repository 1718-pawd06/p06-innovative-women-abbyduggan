Dr.Virginia Apgar (1909-1974)

Early Life

Virginia Apgar was the first female professor at Columbia University's College of Physicians and Surgeons. She was born in Westfield New Jersey on June 7th, 1909, the 3rd child of Charles E. and Helan May Apgar. She's known for her charisma,charm, and most notably, her energy.Many believe her desire to be a doctor came not only from her father's love of astronomy, but also at her brother's chronic illness. Though she was gifted in science, she failed miserably in home economics; her friends even claim she never learned to cook!

Main Content

Apgar attended Mount Holyoke College and graduated with a zoology degree in 1929. She went on to enter Columbia University's College of Physicians and Surgeons, graduating in 1933. She completed her residency in surgery in 1937 and returned to Columbia in 1938, now director of division of anesthesia. She became a professor in 1949 when the study of anesthesia got its own department.Apgar studied the effects of anesthesia on women giving birth to newborn babies. Most notably, she created the Apgar Score, which studies a newborn child's transition from the womb to the outside world. She also lead in the study of birth defects, or teratology, after 1960. She soon became more interested in the study and lead the March of Dime's department of Congenital Malformations.
